Quick Facts!

  • Location: Iloilo City is located on the southeastern part of Panay Island in the Western Visayas region of the Philippines.
  • NameThe name "Iloilo" is thought to be derived from the local phrase "Ilong-Ilong," which means "nose-like." This description is attributed to the shape of the local delta, where the Iloilo River meets the sea, forming an estuary that is said to resemble a nose.

THE CITY OF LOVE

Do you know why Iloilo City is known as the City of Love? Is it because it is the home of Cupid? No! It's because of its reputation for having warm and friendly people. The name refers to the character and hospitality of the locals rather than a romantic connotation. Visitors to Iloilo frequently describe the Ilonggos, or people of Iloilo, as warm, welcoming, and hospitable, contributing to the city's endearing nickname. The people's sense of community and camaraderie is a distinguishing feature of the city, contributing to its reputation as a place where visitors feel welcomed and cared for.


INTRODUCTION

Iloilo City is a wonderful place to live with amazing people. It is nestled in the heart of the Philippines, is a vibrant tapestry woven with history, culture, and culinary delights. Iloilo City, on the Philippine island of Panay, is a bustling urban center known for its economic importance and rich cultural heritage. Iloilo, as a major port city, is vital to trade and commerce. The historical landmarks of the city, such as the Jaro Cathedral and Molo Church, reflect its colonial past. It is also a center for education, with several universities located there. Iloilo City is known for its diverse and delicious local cuisine, which includes specialties like La Paz Batchoy and vibrant festivals like the Dinagyang Festival. Easily accessible through the Iloilo International Airport, the city is a significant destination in the Philippines, offering a blend of history, culture, and economic activity. Word of The Day!

Kanamit!: It literally means "delicious" in English. It's a word you might hear frequently when locals describe delicious food, emphasizing the region's rich culinary culture. It means you enjoyed and appreciated the food; saying "kanamit" to someone's food will brighten their day! Say "kanamit" to your mother's food now!
